Present Perfect Tense

Form

Positive Subject + has/ have + Past Participle
Has = ‘s
Have = ‘ve
She’s done her homework.
Negative Subject + has/ have + not + Past Participle
She has not done her homework.
Question Has/ Have + Subject + Past Participle?
Has she done her homework?

Usages

  • To talk about things which happened in the past, and continue to the present
    E.g. My parents have lived in England for 20 years.

  • To talk about things which happened in the past, but not at a specific time
    E.g. My mom has gone to work.

  • To talk about things we have done many times in the past and continue to do
    E.g. She has practiced the piano since she was 6.

  • To talk about an experience which is connected to the present
    E.g. This is the most beautiful flower I have ever seen.

Signal words

Already, yet, ever, never, just, only just, recently, so far, until now, up to now
